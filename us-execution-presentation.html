09 — Competitive Intelligence
Who we’re up against and why we win. Everyone else stops short.
Comun
THREAT: HIGH
$26M funded. 50x revenue growth. Zero-fee, Spanish-first, 100+ ID types. Same exact target market.
Mitigation: Beat on cross-border + cobranza. No merchant play.
Majority
THREAT: MOD-HIGH
$83.5M+ funded. $5.99/mo subscription. Physical meet-up centers. Broad immigrant focus.
Mitigation: Zero-fee undercuts. Broad = less Latino focus.
Maza
THREAT: MODERATE
a16z backed. $8M. ITIN filing. $150/yr limits adoption.
Mitigation: Add ITIN in Phase 3. Pricing limits scale.
MOAT 01
Cross-Border Network
Existing LATAM user base. Payphone users in Ecuador, Colombia who have family in the U.S. = built-in distribution channel. No U.S. competitor has this.
MOAT 02
Cobranza / Tap to Pay
Zero competitors offer a Spanish-first SoftPOS for Latino micro-merchants in the U.S. Visa Tap to Phone grew 200% YoY. Genuine blue ocean.
MOAT 03
Sports Betting Revenue
No U.S. immigrant neobank has a betting processing vertical. Completely separate revenue line that subsidizes card program growth.
MOAT 04
Ecuador Regulatory Moat
First fintech regulation enacted Sept 2024. Payphone’s home-market position = first-mover advantage while new entrants navigate nascent rules.

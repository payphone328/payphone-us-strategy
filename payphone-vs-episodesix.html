Fundamentals

What They Are

Payphone and Episode Six operate at completely different layers. Payphone is a consumer product; Episode Six is infrastructure that powers products like Payphone.

Payphone (Stripe Architecture)

Consumer/Merchant Fintech Application

Application Layer
  • End-user mobile app for payments, spending, and funding
  • Built entirely on Stripe ecosystem (Connect, Treasury, Issuing, Terminal)
  • Targets Latino immigrants, unbanked/underbanked, small business owners
  • 4 pillars: COBRAR (accept), GASTAR (spend), CARGAR (fund), ENVIAR (remit)
  • No MSB license required — Stripe is the PayFac
  • Revenue: 1% platform fee + 1.5% interchange + cash load fees

Episode Six

Issuer-Processor / Payments Infrastructure

Middleware / Infrastructure Layer
  • API-first processing platform for banks, fintechs, and lenders
  • Real-time RESTful API processing with enterprise-grade compliance
  • Serves global banks, digital banks, fintechs, crypto platforms, governments
  • Full card spectrum: prepaid, debit, credit, commercial, installment, virtual
  • Native ledger engine: virtual accounts, revolving credit, loans, deposits
  • "The World's Local Processor" — built for multi-country from day one

Scenario Analysis

What If Payphone Used Episode Six Instead of Stripe?

Episode Six isn't a competitor to Payphone — it's a potential alternative infrastructure layer. Here's what that tradeoff looks like.

Potential Advantages of E6

  • Full card program control (credit, installments, multi-currency cards)
  • Native ledger flexibility — could build lending, interest-bearing accounts, rewards
  • Less vendor lock-in than Stripe's all-in-one ecosystem
  • Better suited for Phase 2+ global expansion with multi-currency built in
  • Real-time self-service config changes without vendor dependency
  • Could support a BNPL or credit product line in the future

Tradeoffs vs. Current Stripe Architecture

  • Lose Stripe's turnkey PayFac model — would need own BIN sponsor relationship
  • No built-in TAP-to-pay (Terminal SDK) — need separate POS solution
  • No Treasury / Financial Accounts out of the box — need banking partner
  • Significantly more complexity and longer time to market
  • Likely need an MSB license or separate banking partner for money movement
  • No InComm-like cash loading integration included — must build separately
  • Higher upfront cost and integration effort
Recommendation

Bottom Line

Where each platform fits in Payphone's strategy.

Stripe is the Right Call for Phase 1

Your current Stripe-only architecture is the fastest path to market for the U.S. launch. It handles PayFac, PCI, card issuing, treasury, and payment acceptance in a single ecosystem with no MSB license requirement. This lets Payphone focus on the product, user acquisition, and merchant onboarding.

Episode Six Becomes Interesting for Phase 3+

Episode Six is worth bookmarking as Payphone scales beyond what Stripe offers. It becomes relevant when you need:

  • → Credit products or lending (BNPL, installments, revolving credit)
  • → Multi-currency accounts beyond USD
  • → Deeper card program control (commercial cards, custom BINs)
  • → Expansion beyond the Stripe ecosystem's limitations
  • → Native rewards and loyalty programs
Phase 1: Stripe — Ship fast, validate market Phase 2: Stripe + Bridge — Add ENVIAR remittances Phase 3+: Evaluate E6 — Credit, multi-currency, global scale

Target User

Latino immigrants in the U.S. (documented and undocumented). No SSN or ITIN. Currently unbanked or underbanked. Primary language: Spanish. Reliant on cash, check cashing, and informal remittance channels.

1

Consumer Onboarding

  • International gov ID (passport, Matricula, cedula)
  • Proof of U.S. residence
  • Biometric verification (selfie + liveness)
  • Spanish-first UI + English option
  • KYC/CIP compliant
  • Complete in < 5 minutes
2

Card Issuance (Stripe)

  • Virtual card issued instantly via Stripe Issuing
  • Physical card in 5-7 business days
  • Payphone-branded (Naranja #FF6400)
  • Visa or Mastercard (Stripe network)
  • NFC-enabled contactless
3

Account Features

  • FDIC-insured (via Stripe Treasury sponsor bank)
  • Direct deposit (routing + account #)
  • Balance check + transaction history
  • Real-time push notifications
  • Card lock/unlock from app
4

Funding Methods

  • Direct deposit (payroll)
  • Cash at retail (Walmart, CVS, Green Dot)
  • Debit card transfer
  • ACH transfer
5

Spanish-First Support

  • In-app chat in Spanish
  • Phone support in Spanish
  • All comms + legal docs in Spanish
  • English available as secondary
X

NOT in Phase 1

  • Tap to Pay / cobranza
  • Remittances / cross-border
  • Credit building
  • Merchant acquiring
  • ITIN filing assistance

Payphone's Structural Advantages

01

Cross-Border Network

No U.S. competitor has an existing LATAM fintech user base. Payphone users in Ecuador, Colombia who have family in the U.S. = built-in distribution channel.

02

Cobranza / Tap to Pay

Zero competitors offer a Spanish-first SoftPOS for Latino micro-merchants in the U.S. Visa Tap to Phone grew 200% YoY. Genuine blue ocean.

03

Three Revenue Streams

Consumer interchange (Phase 1) + merchant acquiring (Phase 2) + cross-border remittance fees (Phase 3). Plus LATAM betting revenue as a separate business line.

04

Ecuador Regulatory Moat

First fintech regulation enacted Sept 2024. Payphone's home-market position = first-mover advantage while new entrants navigate nascent rules.
